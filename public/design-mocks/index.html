— Vicinity · Site Redesign · v3

Two directions,
benchmarked against the segment.

After auditing five top-tier developer & residence sites — Aman, 111 West 57th (Steinway Tower), 53W53, Naftali Group, Related — three rules emerged: full-bleed hero media, two-typeface system (display serif + tracked all-caps sans), and zero chromatic accent. Both directions below follow them. Both are light.

[ Direction A · The Hotel ]

Quietly considered.
Like a private hotel.

Cream #f3eee7 background, Source Serif 4 doing nearly all the work, warm grey #313131 text. Asymmetric residence grid like an exhibition catalog. Italic pull-quotes, tracked-caps eyebrows, no accent color.

Best when: Vivian's positioning is quiet conviction, off-market access, "buyers come after they've stopped looking on Zillow." Warmest, most distinctive on the page — but the most refined; needs the copy to back it up.
REF: Aman Residences · The Mark Hotel · Cereal Magazine

[ Direction B · The Brokerage ]

Modern, didone,
institutional.

Off-white #fafaf9 + Bodoni Moda + Inter tracked caps. Numbered residence rows like a quarterly portfolio report. Oversized italic Bodoni numerals (01 / 06) on swipe cards. Dense data strip — beds, baths, sf, $/sf — beneath each home.

Best when: Vicinity needs to read as the institution itself, not just a directory. Most data-forward, easiest for sellers to "buy" Vivian's credibility. Slightly colder than A — but better for the $5M+ luxury seller demo.
REF: Naftali Group · 53W53 · Compass Development Marketing
