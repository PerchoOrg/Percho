# Discovery Questions — KW Atlanta Meetup

**Rule:** you are here to *listen*, not to pitch. If you catch yourself
talking more than 40% of the time in any conversation, stop. Ask another
question. The best outcome tonight is a notebook full of quotes, not a
stack of signups.

Ten open-ended questions, roughly ordered from easiest-to-answer to
most-revealing. Pick 3–5 per conversation — don't run through all ten like
a survey.

---

### 1. Warm-up

**"How long have you been with Keller Williams, and what part of Atlanta
do you mostly work?"**
*(Establishes rapport, tells you buyer- vs listing-side, and gives you a
neighborhood to reference later.)*

---

### 2. Current buyer sourcing

**"Where are your buyers coming from right now — referrals, Zillow, open
houses, social?"**
*(Get their real mix. Follow up: what percentage from each?)*

---

### 3. Zillow reality check

**"What's the single most frustrating thing about Zillow leads for you
right now?"**
*(This is the big one. Do NOT interrupt. Let them vent. Write down the
verbatim words.)*

---

### 4. Cost of doing business

**"Roughly what are you spending per closed buyer to get a lead — Zillow,
FUB, ads, whatever?"**
*(Number may be fuzzy. That's fine. What you want is whether they think
about it as a cost line or as "just what you do.")*

---

### 5. Video comfort

**"How do you feel about being on camera for listings? Love it, hate it,
tolerate it?"**
*(Screens out agents for whom "upload one video" is a non-starter. Also
tells you tone for follow-up.)*

---

### 6. Existing social attempts

**"Have you tried Instagram Reels or TikTok for your listings? What
happened?"**
*(Almost every agent has tried. Almost none saw ROI. The gap between
"posted a Reel" and "closed a deal from a Reel" is our whole thesis.)*

---

### 7. Buyer behavior observation

**"When your buyers come to you, how are they finding houses before they
call you? Are they on Zillow, Redfin, TikTok, driving around?"**
*(Agents watch buyer behavior all day. Their answer is more accurate than
any consumer survey we could run.)*

---

### 8. New-tool threshold

**"When a new platform pitches you, what would actually make you try it —
versus filing it under 'I'll get to it'?"**
*(Directly informs our onboarding friction and what "free during beta"
should include. Look for: ease of setup, someone else vouching, low time
commitment, no exclusivity.)*

---

### 9. Broker & compliance

**"How does your broker feel about you being on new platforms? Is
compliance a hurdle, or do you have latitude?"**
*(KW agents have relatively high autonomy, but this varies by market
center. Tells you whether you need to build a broker-facing pitch too.)*

---

### 10. Referral / community

**"If Percho turned out to actually work for you, who's the first
Atlanta agent friend you'd tell?"**
*(Two purposes: (a) tests real interest — a lukewarm agent won't name
anyone, (b) surfaces the real influencers in the KW Atlanta network.
Only ask this once you've earned some interest — don't lead with it.)*

---

## After each conversation

Jot down within 60 seconds:

- **Name + brokerage:** ____________________
- **Top pain quote (verbatim if possible):** "____________________"
- **Follow-up? Y/N:** _____
- **Warm/lukewarm/cold:** _____

Move that into the notes template at the end of the night.

---

## What NOT to do

- Don't argue when they trash-talk Zillow. Just nod and take notes.
- Don't correct their misconception about Percho mid-vent — write it down
  and address it in the follow-up email.
- Don't offer discounts, exclusive deals, or special treatment on the spot.
  We don't have pricing to discount.
- Don't ask them to sign up on your phone unless *they* ask to. QR + card
  is enough tonight.
