# Percho Q&A Playbook — KW Atlanta Meetup

Anticipated agent questions with concise, truthful answers. When in doubt,
under-promise. If you don't know, say "I don't know yet — that's the kind of
question I came here to hear."

---

## Pricing & terms

**Q: What does it cost?**
Free during beta. No signup fee, no monthly, no per-lead charge. When we
introduce pricing later, beta agents get grandfathered terms.

**Q: Is there a contract?**
No. Sign up, upload, leave whenever. No exclusivity, no minimum term.

**Q: Are you going to end up like Zillow — reselling my leads back to me?**
No. That model is exactly what we're not building. Every lead a buyer
generates on Percho goes 100% to the agent whose listing they contacted.
We do not resell, share, auction, or route buyer contacts to third parties.

---

## Exclusivity & channel conflict

**Q: Do I have to pull my listing from Zillow / Redfin / Realtor.com?**
No. Percho is non-exclusive and additive. Keep every current channel.

**Q: Can I use my own listing videos I already shot for Instagram/TikTok?**
Yes. As long as you have the rights to the video, upload whatever you have.
One vertical walkthrough is ideal (~30–90 seconds), but we accept longer.

---

## Lead ownership & data

**Q: Who owns the leads?**
The agent. When a buyer taps "contact agent," we hand off name + contact
info to you and log it in your dashboard. We don't retain a lead-broker
relationship in the middle.

**Q: Do you sell buyer data to anyone?**
No. Buyer signups are used to run Percho — matching, notifications,
support. Not sold.

**Q: Who owns the video I upload?**
You do. You grant Percho a license to display it on our platform while
the listing is active. Take it down anytime and the license ends.

---

## Product vs Zillow / Redfin

**Q: How is this different from Zillow?**
Three things:

1. **Format:** video-first, swipe-based feed — not a photo grid + search
   filters. Buyers *discover* listings the way they discover TikToks.
2. **Branding:** every listing card shows the agent's face, name, license,
   brokerage. On Zillow the agent is often invisible until the "premier
   agent" ad slot loads.
3. **Lead economics:** leads go straight to the listing agent, no resale.

**Q: How is this different from Redfin?**
Redfin is a brokerage first, portal second. We're not a brokerage. Percho
doesn't compete for the buyer — the agent does.

**Q: Isn't this just Instagram Reels for houses?**
Instagram doesn't know a buyer's price band, neighborhood shortlist, or
that they're actually house-hunting. Percho does — because the content
*is* real listings with structured MLS data behind every clip. Matching is
the point.

## Data source & compliance

**Q: Where does the listing data come from?**
FMLS (First Multiple Listing Service). We access it via Bridge Interactive,
FMLS's official RESO Web API vendor.

**Q: Are you IDX-compliant?**
Yes — today we operate under IDX-style display rules for public listings.
Attribution, refresh cadence, and take-down handling all follow FMLS rules.

**Q: What about VOW?**
VOW-side access is on our roadmap but not live yet. When it lights up,
we'll gate the extra data behind buyer registration per FMLS rules.

**Q: My broker is going to ask about compliance. Who do they call?**
Give them my card — [FOUNDER NAME], [EMAIL]. Happy to jump on a call with
your broker or compliance officer directly.

## Video specifics

**Q: What kind of video works?**
Vertical (9:16), 30–90 seconds is the sweet spot. Walk-through, hero shots,
you talking to camera at the end — whatever feels natural. Phone footage is
fine; production polish helps but isn't required.

**Q: File size / format?**
Up to a few hundred MB, common formats (mp4, mov). We transcode on our end.

**Q: Can I upload multiple videos per listing?**
MVP is one primary video per listing. Multi-video is on the roadmap.

## Signup & onboarding

**Q: How do I sign up?**
Scan our QR or go to **percho.co/agents**. Fill in name, email, phone,
brokerage, MLS association. We verify your license and FMLS membership,
then send you a dashboard invite. Usually 1–2 business days during beta.

**Q: Do I need a Percho buyer account too?**
No — separate agent dashboard. Web-based today.

**Q: What happens after I upload my first listing?**
It appears in the swipe feed for buyers matching that listing's
neighborhood + price band. You'll see impressions and taps in your
dashboard, and any buyer contact requests come to your email + dashboard.

---

## How buyers find & contact you

**Q: How does a buyer actually reach me?**
Two taps on the listing card: "Contact agent" opens a form (name, phone,
message). Submit sends it to your email + dashboard immediately. Buyers can
also request a tour with proposed times.

**Q: Is there a chat?**
In-app chat is on the roadmap. Today it's email + phone handoff.

---

## Coverage & roadmap

**Q: What markets are you in?**
Atlanta metro only right now — MVP is intentionally narrow. FMLS-covered
areas.

**Q: When are you expanding?**
When Atlanta agents tell us they'd refer a peer. Not before. Probably other
Southeast metros next.

**Q: Is there an app?**
Web today, iOS in beta, Android after. Buyers can use it on mobile web
without installing anything.

## Objection catches

**Q: "I don't have time to shoot videos."**
Totally fair. One phone-shot walkthrough per listing is the ask — most
agents already do this for Instagram. If that's still too much, Percho
isn't for you today, and I'd rather you know that upfront.

**Q: "I've heard this pitch from ten startups."**
I know. I'm not asking you to switch anything or pay anything. I'm asking
for your email and, if you're up for it, 15 minutes next week to tell me
where our agent flow breaks.

**Q: "Prove you'll still be around in a year."**
Fair. We're funded, small, and Atlanta-focused. If we're gone in a year,
you've lost zero dollars and one video upload. That's the deal.
