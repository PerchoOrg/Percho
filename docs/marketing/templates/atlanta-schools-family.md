## The subdivision data blocks (pick 2-3 per reply, never all)

**Sources:** GreatSchools ratings pulled 2026-06 via the k12 pipeline
(scripts/k12/upsert_schools.py). Commute times = personal experience +
Google Maps at 8am weekday. Prices = Zillow median band ± informal read
of current market. Anything else — hedge with "not 100% sure".

### Peachtree Corners (Gwinnett)

- **Schools (Berkeley Lake ES / Pinckneyville MS / Norcross HS):**
  ES around GS-8, MS GS-6, HS GS-5. Not the top-top but solid & diverse.
  Simpson ES specifically is GS-9 (that's the one we're in).
- **Commute:** ~25min to Perimeter, 30-35min to Buckhead via 141 → 400
- **Price band:** $500-750k for 3-4br in a decent sub
- **HOA:** varies wildly, $600-1500/yr in most subs
- **Vibe:** big Indian & Chinese community, active downtown (Town Center),
  tons of restaurants. Not walkable overall but pocket-walkable
- **Tradeoff to name:** Norcross HS test scores are meh compared to
  Northview / Johns Creek. If HS ranking is the north star, this is not it.

### Johns Creek (North Fulton)

- **Schools:** Northview HS is one of the top public HS in GA (GS-10).
  Elementary options are strong across the board.
- **Commute:** Buckhead is rough — 45-60min on 400 at peak. Perimeter
  25-30min.
- **Price band:** $650-950k for what P'tree Corners has at $550k
- **HOA:** typically higher, $1200-2500/yr, some subs have swim/tennis
- **Vibe:** heavy Korean & Indian community, safest feeling of the metro
  imo, but very car-dependent, very homogeneously affluent
- **Tradeoff:** commute + price. You pay a lot for the school rating.

### Alpharetta / Milton

- **Schools:** Cambridge HS (Milton) and Alpharetta HS both GS-9-ish.
  Milton schools slightly edge out Alpharetta on average.
- **Commute:** worst of the three for Buckhead. 50-70min on bad days.
- **Price band:** $700k-$1.2M for the "good" subs. Under $600k gets hard.
- **HOA:** $800-2000/yr
- **Vibe:** Avalon (mall/mixed-use) is genuinely walkable, downtown
  Alpharetta cute. Milton is horsey/estate feel.
- **Tradeoff:** commute + you're pretty far from anywhere ITP.

### East Cobb (Marietta)

- **Schools:** Walton HS + Pope HS both GS-9-10. Historically the "old
  money" top schools of the metro.
- **Commute:** to Buckhead 25-40min via 75, more predictable than 400.
- **Price band:** $600-900k
- **HOA:** typically low or none, older subs
- **Vibe:** less diverse than Gwinnett / N Fulton, more classic
  suburban-American. Some fantastic parks (Kennesaw Mountain nearby).
- **Tradeoff:** less new construction, some subs feel dated

### Sandy Springs (if OP wants ITP-ish)

- **Schools:** mixed. North Springs HS is a magnet-heavy public that
  can be great if kid is in the magnet; regular track less standout.
- **Commute:** Buckhead 15-25min — closest of any option here
- **Price band:** $700k-$1.5M
- **HOA:** varies, condo territory too
- **Vibe:** more walkable pockets than the others, mix of townhome / condo
  / SFH. Feels more urban.
- **Tradeoff:** schools are the weakest of the list on paper. Some
  parents supplement w/ private middle school

### Decatur (City of, not DeKalb)

- **Schools:** City Schools of Decatur is highly rated across the board,
  small district
- **Commute:** to Buckhead 20-30min, to Midtown 15min
- **Price band:** $650k-$1.3M for something w/ a yard
- **HOA:** most old subs no HOA
- **Vibe:** the walkable / bike-friendly / crunchy pick. Farmers markets,
  indie coffee, actual sidewalks.
- **Tradeoff:** small district, competitive to get in the right zone,
  house sizes generally smaller than Gwinnett/N Fulton
